### Q4.1

**Induction Hypothesis:** Assume for all hands \( h' \) and accumulators \( acc \), \( add\_up(h') + acc = add\_up\_tr(h', acc) \).

**Base Case:** When \( h = Empty \):  
- \( add\_up(Empty) = 0 \)  
- \( add\_up\_tr(Empty, acc) = acc \)  
- Therefore, \( add\_up(Empty) + acc = 0 + acc = acc \)  
Thus, \( add\_up(Empty) + acc = add\_up\_tr(Empty, acc) \).

**Step Case:** When \( h = Hand(c, h') \):  
- By the induction hypothesis, assume \( add\_up(h') + acc = add\_up\_tr(h', acc) \). 
- Then:  
  \( add\_up(Hand(c, h')) = add\_up(h') + 1 \)  
  \( add\_up\_tr(Hand(c, h'), acc) = add\_up\_tr(h', acc + 1) \)  
- Now, using the induction hypothesis:
  \( add\_up(h') + acc + 1 = add\_up\_tr(h', acc) + 1 \)  
Which reduces to:
  \( add\_up(h') + 1 = add\_up\_tr(h', acc + 1) \).

### Q4.2

**Theorem 2 (Main Result):** For all hands \( h \), \( add\_up(h) = add\_up\_tr(h, 0) \).

**Proof:** By applying Theorem 1 with \( acc = 0 \):  
From the induction hypothesis for all \( h \), we have:
- \( add\_up(h) + 0 = add\_up\_tr(h, 0) \) implies \( add\_up(h) = add\_up\_tr(h, 0) \).

Thus, by structural induction, \( add\_up(h) = add\_up\_tr(h, 0) \) for all hands \( h \).
