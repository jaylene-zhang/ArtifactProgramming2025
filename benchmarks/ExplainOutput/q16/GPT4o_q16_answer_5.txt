**Solution:**  
`shift_point 2` will return `(7, 6)`.

**Explanation:**  
- Initially, `point` is defined as `(4, 3)`.
- The definition `let point = shift_point 1` evaluates `shift_point 1`:
  - `component_x point` gives `4`, and `component_y point` gives `3`.
  - `shift_point 1` calculates `(4 + 1, 3 + 1)` which results in `(5, 4)`.
  - The `point` is now updated to `(5, 4)`.
- When `shift_point 2` is called:
  - `component_x point` gives `5`, and `component_y point` gives `4` (due to the updated `point`).
  - `shift_point 2` calculates `(5 + 2, 4 + 2)` which results in `(7, 6)`.
