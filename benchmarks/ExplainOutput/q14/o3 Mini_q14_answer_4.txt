Solution:
A function is tail-recursive when its recursive call is the last action, meaning no work is done after the call. For example:

------------------------------------------------
let rec tail_sum lst acc =
  match lst with
  | [] -> acc
  | h :: t -> tail_sum t (acc + h)

let sum lst = tail_sum lst 0
------------------------------------------------

Explanation:
In tail_sum, the recursive call tail_sum t (acc + h) is the final operation performed. Thereâ€™s no pending computation once the recursive call is made, allowing the compiler to optimize the recursion into iteration and use constant stack space. This contrasts with non-tail-recursive functions where additional operations follow the recursive call, consuming extra stack memory.
